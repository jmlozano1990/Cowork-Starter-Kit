# v2.9.0 "Dynamic Reclaim" Research Memo (2026-07-18)

**Purpose:** feed the v2.9.0 Phase 0 spec. Two research tracks: (1) an internal drift trace establishing what changed, when, and why the current wizard reads as preset-first rather than co-creative; (2) external research on conversational onboarding, draft-then-refine UX, and choice-overload mitigation, to ground the reframe in evidence rather than taste.

---

## Part 1 — Internal drift trace (what actually happened)

### The v1.2 north star

ADR-010/011/012 (2026-04-17) established the original "Dynamic Workspace Architect" vision: a wizard that discovers the user's goal in open-ended conversation, rather than presenting a fixed menu. ADR-010's own rationale for rejecting a starter-file/CLAUDE.md split ("Option A") is instructive: a two-tier experience where some users get a lesser, more form-like wizard was called "a critical failure mode" for a product targeting non-technical users. The same standard applies here — a wizard that snaps confident users to a preset while making custom-goal users feel like they took the harder, lesser path is the same failure mode in a different place.

### The router's origin (v2.4.0, then re-scoped v2.6.0)

ADR-021's v2.4 amendment (2026-05-08) replaced the original fixed 7-item Q1 menu with the 3-path keyword router (Path A preset match / Path B overlap / Path C custom). At inception, the router's own worked-example pseudocode (`docs/architecture.md` OQ-1, v2.4) used a `≥2` match threshold. Security framing was established at the same time: goal text is DATA for keyword matching only (C-v2.4-6), and F4 additions are bounded to the existing skill pool (C-v2.4-7) — both are non-negotiable and **out of scope for this cycle to touch**.

v2.6.0 ("Dynamic Preset Scaffolds," re-scoped 2026-05-10) introduced the `core_skills` / `optional_skills` / `cross_cutting_skills` tiered schema still in use today, and the runtime skill-swap affordance. This is the direct precedent for **re-scoping a numbered version slot on an owner's explicit call** — v2.6 was publicly committed as multi-tool authoring; the owner flipped it to a preset-audit finding instead, with multi-tool deferred forward. v2.9.0 "Dynamic Reclaim" follows the identical pattern: the roadmap-committed "Distribution & Trust" content is not abandoned, it moves to v2.10.

### The actual drift (v2.7.0, commit `e2f622d`, 2026-07-06)

Direct inspection of the commit (`git show e2f622d`) shows a real, well-reasoned bug fix that also imported unreviewed product-shaping language:

- **Fixed a real bug:** pre-fix, Path A required `≥3` matching signals with an 8-token vocabulary and no stemming — the commit message states this "was ... which no natural one-sentence goal reaches." This is independently corroborated by `docs/research/v2.7-usercase-test-and-improvement-research.md` (the pre-fix swarm test, dated 2026-07-06): 5 of 7 personas' natural goals scored 0–1 signals against a `≥3` bar ("I'm studying for my biochemistry finals" = 1 Study signal), and the research explicitly ranked "fix F3 routing" as the #6 highest-value improvement, judged 8.0/10 across user-value/feasibility/governance lenses.
- **Also introduced, unreviewed:** the "judgment tie-break" mechanism and its justifying language — "a wrong suggestion costs one 'no', while a false Path C costs the whole scaffold." This sentence is the one the owner has now flagged: it frames Path C (custom composition) as an expensive failure mode to be avoided, and Path A (preset match) as the cheap, safe default. The tie-break itself is one-directional — it only ever escalates a Path-C-scoring goal *up* to Path A, never the reverse — which is exactly the mechanism that produces "3 of 4 entry-input classes snap to a preset."

Both changes shipped in the same commit, out-of-pipeline (no Phase 0–7 record), which is why the cost-framing language was never gated by the owner as a product decision — it rode in on a legitimate bug fix.

### What must not regress

`docs/research/v2.7-usercase-test-and-improvement-research.md` documents specific, previously-shipped defects this cycle must not reintroduce: interview abandonment risk (>50% abandon at 10+ questions — FICO, n=14k, cited in that research), the fast-track exit that used to leave no files on disk (fixed by the F4 checkpoint-stub, now load-bearing), and Path C misrouting of goals that plainly fit one preset (the exact bug `e2f622d` fixed). The v2.9.0 rework changes **framing and structural parity**, not the `≥2` threshold, the 16-signal vocabulary, or the stemming rule — those are the corrected mechanics, not the drift.

## Part 2 — External research: co-creation, drafts, and choice without overload

Six vendor-neutral sources, all directly applicable to a setup wizard that offers a preset match as a starting point rather than a verdict:

1. **Nielsen, J. (2006, updated). "Progressive Disclosure."** Nielsen Norman Group. `https://www.nngroup.com/articles/progressive-disclosure/` — The canonical formulation: show the most important options first, defer the rest to an explicit request. NN/g's own usability research finds designs beyond 2 disclosure levels "typically have low usability" — relevant to keeping the "why this draft" reasoning to one short line, not a reasoning trace.

2. **Nielsen, J. (2026-07-09). "Progressive Disclosure: From Training Wheels to Week-Long AI Agents."** `https://jakobnielsenphd.substack.com/p/progressive-disclosure` — Applies the 2006 principle to modern agentic UX: "Layer AI answers. Verdict first, in 1 short paragraph; reasoning, sources, and detail behind disclosure controls." Directly supports showing a one-line "matched: finals" reasoning alongside a preset draft, rather than either hiding the match logic entirely (which reads as arbitrary) or over-explaining it (which adds friction the owner explicitly ruled out).

3. **Campbell, E. (2025). "Draft Mode" AI UX Pattern.** ShapeofAI.com (CC-BY-NC-SA). `https://www.shapeof.ai/patterns/draft-mode` — "Users should never be surprised when the system produces a lower-scaled version of what they expect"; drafts should make explicit what's provisional and keep "upgrade" / "adjust" one step away. This is the direct design vocabulary for reframing Path A/B from a yes/no confirm into an explicit, adjustable draft.

4. **Griciūnas, A. (2026-03-11). "Agent Skills: Progressive Disclosure as a System Design Pattern."** swirlai newsletter. `https://www.newsletter.swirlai.com/p/agent-skills-progressive-disclosure` — Describes the exact three-layer discovery/activation/execution model Claude Agent Skills already use, and states the core principle plainly: "show only what is needed for the immediate task and defer everything else." Directly applicable to Path C's pool-composition step: surface a small curated starting set with an explicit, cheap "want more" affordance, rather than either dumping the full pool or artificially capping it as a lesser feature.

5. **Mantlr Editorial (2026-05-26). "Designing for AI Agents: 10 UX Patterns Every Designer Needs in 2026."** `https://mantlr.com/blog/designing-for-ai-agents-ux-patterns-2026` — Pattern 4, "The Action Preview": present what will happen before it happens, and make it editable — "if the user can tweak the agent's output before execution, they feel like a collaborator." Pattern 3, "Explainability on Demand," reinforces the summary-then-detail structure. This is the clearest external articulation of "collaborator, not approver" — the exact distinction between the current binary "is that right?" and the co-creation framing this cycle restores.

6. **Iyengar, S. S., & Lepper, M. R. (2000).** *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*, "When Choice is Demotivating" (the "jam study"), summarized via Coglode Research (`https://www.coglode.com/research/choice-paradox`) — the foundational finding that 6 curated options outperformed 24 on both conversion (40% vs. 3%) and satisfaction. This is the evidence base for keeping Path C's *initial* suggestion count small (3, expandable) rather than either the old flat "≤3 forever" cap or an uncapped full-pool dump — the fix is removing the *cost framing* and the *structural asymmetry* with Path A/B, not removing the small-first-screen principle, which is itself well-supported.

**Synthesis for this spec:** none of these sources argue for "show everything" as the antidote to feeling shrunk. They converge on the same mechanism this cycle should use: keep the *first screen* small and fast (progressive disclosure, choice-overload research), but make it explicitly a **draft** — editable, reasoned, and structurally equal whether the starting point is a preset or a blank pool composition (draft-mode, action-preview literature). The v2.7 fix already got the matching mechanics right; what's missing is exactly this framing layer, and it is well-supported external practice, not a house-style guess.
