# v2.10.0 "Empowerment Skills" Research Memo (2026-07-19)

**Purpose:** feed the v2.10.0 Phase 0 spec. Owner directive (2026-07-18, verbatim): "I want to be sure that we do also have 'additional' skills to add that can be useful. Like Anti AI slop, config the user voice if needed, any other thing that empowers Cowork should be possible to be done to increase the experience! The call of the user but we do offer." Two research tracks: (1) an internal audit of the pool/registry/preset/CI state this cycle inherits, so the candidate slate is grounded in what already exists rather than duplicating it; (2) external research on AI-slop detection (to make the anti-slop skill evidence-based, not vibes) and on skill categories that empower non-technical knowledge workers in AI workspaces, to ground the "any other thing that empowers Cowork" half of the directive in more than taste.

## Part 1 — Internal audit: what this cycle inherits

### Pool, registry, and offer-architecture state (verified this session)

- **23-skill pool** (`skills/<slug>/SKILL.md`), all 9-section template-compliant (ADR-015), 60-line CI floor (ADR-016), `tools: [claude-code]` frontmatter gate (ADR-029 MF-3). `curated-skills-registry.md` carries 24 rows across the 23 unique slugs (`research-synthesis` has an intentional Study/Research dual-listing, ADR-018) — its own footnote states this count explicitly and is one of the places this cycle's row additions must update.
- **Three independent offer surfaces**, none of which require a new interview turn to extend:
  1. **`cross_cutting_skills:`** (selection-presets.md, pool-level annotation, currently 5: action-items, meeting-notes, doc-summary, voice-matching, research-synthesis) — offered at F4's "Add from cross-cutting" (up to 3 shown at a time; WIZARD.md does not hardcode the list length, so growing it from 5 to 7 is a zero-prose-change addition).
  2. **Preset `optional_skills:`** — surfaced at Q1 Path A/B ("Also on the bench, yours to add now or mid-session later") and at F4's "Add from optional tier." Unlike `core_skills`, `optional_skills` members are NOT byte-mirrored into `examples/<preset>/.claude/skills/` and are NOT covered by the `skill-depth-check` CMP job (that job's loop is explicitly scoped to each preset's `core_skills` list, per `.github/workflows/quality.yml`'s `CMP — Byte-mirror assertion` step) — so adding to `optional_skills` is materially cheaper CI-surface than adding to `core_skills`.
  3. **Path C `goal_tags` matching + F4 "Add from full pool"** (WIZARD.md WS-COMPOSITION, shipped v2.9.0) — any pool skill with populated `goal_tags` is automatically discoverable for a crossover/novel goal without any preset placement at all. Registry `goal_tags` coverage was confirmed 100% (24/24 rows) at the v2.9.0 Phase 1 production-validation.
- **`core_skills:` is explicitly out of scope** for this cycle by owner directive, and is also the highest-CI-cost tier (CMP byte-mirror requires a matching `examples/<preset>/.claude/skills/<slug>/SKILL.md` file, byte-identical to the pool file). No candidate in this memo's slate proposes a `core_skills` change.
- **One instructive non-precedent:** `prompt-gate` is a pool skill and a registry row, but appears in **no** preset's `core_skills`/`optional_skills` and **not** in `cross_cutting_skills` — it is not referenced anywhere in `WIZARD.md`'s installable-bundle flow. It ships implicitly (v2.9.0 spec's own AC-P1-4 binds "prompt-gate stays implicit — 0 references in selection-presets.md"). This is a *different* distribution shape from what the owner's directive asks for here: prompt-gate is a silent, always-available utility; the owner's framing for this cycle ("the call of the user but we do offer") is explicitly opt-in and visible. This memo does not recommend the prompt-gate pattern for any v2.10.0 candidate — see the anti-ai-slop tier discussion in the spec for why an always-on rewrite pass would be the wrong shape for output-altering content.

### Choice-overload precedent already established in this codebase

v2.9.0's own research memo cites Iyengar & Lepper's "jam study" (6 curated options beat 24 on both conversion and satisfaction) as the reason Path C's *initial* suggestion count stays small-then-expandable rather than dumping the full pool. That finding governs this cycle's own restraint: the candidate slate below is capped at 6 entries (not "every idea considered"), and the tier-placement recommendations favor `cross_cutting_skills`/`optional_skills`/`goal_tags` additions over growing any single preset's always-shown surface.

### Adjacent dispositions already on record (do not re-litigate)

`curated-skills-registry.md`'s own Disposition Annotations mark `doc-summary` and `action-items` **`covered-by-runtime`** — "no in-tree expansion planned" (source: `docs/internal/process/skills-roadmap.md` §Section 1). Any new candidate whose JTBD substantially overlaps either of those stays out of this cycle's slate; this ruled out an early "information-diet triage" idea from being framed as a doc-summary variant (see spec §Candidate Slate item 6 disposition).

### CI/prose surfaces that hard-code the pool count (enumerated, not assumed)

A repo-wide scan for `23-skill`/`23 skills`/`(23 slugs)` found the count hard-coded in **prose, not in CI logic**, at seven live (non-historical) locations: `WIZARD.md` (three occurrences, two of which carry the security-relevant C-v2.4-6/C-v2.4-7 pool-boundary language), `SETUP-CHECKLIST.md`, `README.md`, `templates/workspace-claude-md-template.md` (which becomes the user's own post-handover `CLAUDE.md`), and `tests/offline-smoke-test.md`. Two further occurrences in `CHANGELOG.md` are historical release notes and are correctly excluded (append-only). `.github/workflows/quality.yml`'s `skill-depth-check` job carries one descriptive comment ("Iterates over `skills/*/SKILL.md` (23 files)") that is stale-but-harmless — the job's actual loop is `for skill_file in skills/*/SKILL.md` (self-adjusting, no hard-coded count) and `registry-cardinality-check` enforces a **floor** of 18, not an exact count — so no CI *logic* requires a change; only the comment and the seven prose locations do. This distinction (logic vs. comment vs. prose) is the basis for this cycle's classification proposal — see spec §Classification.

## Part 2 — External research, Track A: what "AI slop" actually is (evidence for the anti-slop skill)

Five sources, chosen to ground the anti-ai-slop skill's `## Anti-patterns` and `## Quality criteria` sections in named, checkable tells rather than a vague "sounds AI-generated" heuristic. All accessed 2026-07-19.

1. **aicheckr.io (2026). "What Is AI Slop? Examples & How to Detect It."** `https://www.aicheckr.io/blog/what-is-ai-slop-and-how-to-detect-it` — Names the recurring vocabulary tier directly: "delve," "tapestry," "landscape" (used metaphorically), "crucial," "pivotal," "seamless," "robust," "leverage," "elevate," "navigate" (metaphorically), "in the realm of," "it's important to note." Also documents the "it's not just X, it's Y" symmetrical-construction tic and generic scene-setting openers ("In today's fast-paced world").
2. **aicheckr.io (2026). "AI Slop Examples: 12 Before & After Fixes."** `https://www.aicheckr.io/blog/ai-slop-examples` — Concrete before/after pairs for the same tell categories; useful as the shape for the skill's own `## Example` section (a worked "before: slop / after: de-slopped" pair, matching the citation-formatter/editing-pass precedent of one real worked example).
3. **Duey AI (2026). "The Em-Dash Myth: What Actually Gives Away AI Writing."** `https://www.duey.ai/post/em-dash-ai-writing` — Important corrective, not just confirmation: the em dash itself is not the tell — *density* is (twelve-plus in 500 words, combined with uniform section breaks and repetitive sentence structure). This directly informs an anti-pattern the skill must avoid: flagging every em dash regardless of the sample's own established density, which would make the skill actively hostile to writers (like this repo's own `voice-matching` skill) whose natural register uses em dashes at a real, human density. The anti-slop skill's design must inherit `voice-matching`'s existing "match sample density, don't impose AI-default density" principle rather than contradict it.
4. **GPTZero (2026). "What Is Perplexity & Burstiness for AI Detection?"** `https://gptzero.me/news/perplexity-and-burstiness-what-is-it/` — The technical grounding for "uniform sentence rhythm" as a tell: human writing is "bursty" (short, punchy sentences interleaved with long, complex ones); AI output clusters sentence length in a narrow range because the model applies consistent probabilistic rules. This gives the skill a concrete, describable check ("does the passage vary sentence length, or does every sentence run 18–22 words") rather than an unfalsifiable "feels robotic."
5. **Hastewire (2026). "Uncover Linguistic Patterns of AI Writing: Key Tells."** `https://hastewire.com/blog/uncover-linguistic-patterns-of-ai-writing-key-tells` — Corroborates hedging-without-commitment ("might potentially help," "generally speaking," "in many cases") as a distinct, separately-checkable category from vocabulary and rhythm — useful because it means the skill's `## Quality criteria` can enumerate 3 independent categories (vocabulary, rhythm/burstiness, hedging) rather than one vague pass/fail.

**Synthesis for the slate:** the anti-slop skill has three independently checkable categories with real evidence behind them — a named vocabulary denylist, a rhythm/burstiness check, and a hedging-language check — plus one explicit **anti-anti-pattern**: never flag a device (em dash, short-paragraph structure, a hedge) that the sample text or the user's own `context/writing-profile.md` establishes as their real, intentional style. That last point is the difference between "authenticity pass" and "AI-detector cosplay," and it is why the skill must read `context/writing-profile.md` when present (same integration contract `voice-matching`/`editing-pass` already use) rather than applying a fixed denylist blind to the user's own voice.

## Part 3 — External research, Track B: what genuinely empowers a non-technical knowledge worker in an AI workspace

Four sources, chosen against the owner's "any other thing that empowers Cowork" framing — deliberately screened against Cowork's actual shape (offline-first at wizard-time, file-based, no live web fetch inside a skill) rather than generic "AI productivity" content. All accessed 2026-07-19.

1. **Emel Hamilton (2026). "Prompt Hygiene Is the New Bedside Skill for the AI Era."** LinkedIn. `https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/prompt-hygiene-new-bedside-skill-ai-era-emel-hamilton-cfqwe` — Frames precise, context-rich prompting as a distinct, learnable communication skill, not an engineering skill. **Disposition: not a new candidate** — this exact JTBD is already shipped in-tree as `prompt-gate` (enrich vague prompts, ask ≤3 grounded clarifying questions, workspace-context-aware). Cited here specifically to document that the "prompt hygiene" empowerment category from the owner's brief is already covered, so the slate doesn't propose a duplicate.
2. **Asian Efficiency (2026). "GTD Weekly Review: Step-by-Step Guide + Checklist."** `https://www.asianefficiency.com/productivity/gtd-weekly-review/` — The canonical Collect → Process → Review → Plan weekly-review structure. Distinct from this pool's existing periodic-summary skills: `daily-briefing` is same-day and forward-looking only; `status-update`/`risk-assessment` are project-audience-calibrated, not a personal zoom-out; `follow-up-tracker` is commitment-specific, not a full review. No existing skill performs a periodic (weekly-cadence) zoom-out across a user's own files. This is the evidence base for the `weekly-review` candidate.
3. **Mem0 (2026). "Context Engineering AI: How to Build Smarter LLM Agents in 2026."** `https://mem0.ai/blog/context-engineering-ai-agents-guide` — On memory hygiene specifically: "don't extract copies of what's already in your static knowledge base into agent memory — extract only what's interaction-specific: preferences, decisions, corrections, commitments." This is directly useful evidence for *scoping* a would-be "decision log" candidate narrowly (durable decision+rationale capture, not a second copy of meeting-notes' full transcript extraction) — see the decision-log entry in the candidate slate, recommended **defer**, not built this cycle, precisely because getting that narrow scope right deserves its own research pass rather than being squeezed into this cycle's remaining budget.
4. **Sourcegraph (2026). "Context Engineering: A Practical Guide for AI Agents."** `https://sourcegraph.com/blog/context-engineering` — Reinforces the same point from a different angle (context is a designed, curated surface, not "log everything") and documents the general 2026 shift from ad hoc prompting toward structured, purpose-scoped context. Used here as corroboration, not as the basis for a new skill on its own — Cowork's `context/writing-profile.md` and `cowork-profile.md` already implement a purpose-scoped context file per-workspace; this research supports *not* over-building a generic "memory management" skill on top of that (no candidate in the slate proposes one), since the existing profile-file architecture already is the context-engineering pattern for this product, just under different names.

**Synthesis for the slate:** the two strongest, most evidence-backed genuinely-new candidates are **weekly-review** (real JTBD gap, well-established external methodology, cheap to scope narrowly) and, from the owner's own seed, **anti-ai-slop** (real, checkable evidence base, clean differentiation from `voice-matching`/`editing-pass` once scoped as a universal last-pass rather than a writing-preset-only tool). A **decision-log** idea surfaced from the context-engineering research is real but is recommended for deferral — its scoping risk (durable decision capture vs. redundant restatement of `meeting-notes`/`risk-assessment`/`follow-up-tracker` content) is exactly the kind of judgment call that benefits from a dedicated research pass rather than a rushed fit into an already-multi-candidate cycle. Full slate, tier placements, and build/defer recommendations are in `docs/spec.md` §v2.10.0.
